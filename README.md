# FUTURE_CS_07
# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

## Project Overview
This project focuses on identifying phishing emails and spreading awareness about common phishing techniques. Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers attempt to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data through deceptive emails.

The objective of this project is to analyze email samples, identify phishing indicators, classify emails based on risk level, and provide prevention guidelines for users.

This task is part of the Cyber Security Internship program.

---

## Objectives

• Identify phishing indicators such as spoofed senders, fake domains, and malicious links  
• Classify emails as Safe, Suspicious, or Phishing  
• Explain phishing threats in simple business-friendly language  
• Provide prevention and awareness guidelines for users  

---

## Tools Used

• Email header analyzers  
• Browser tools  
• Microsoft Word / Google Docs for documentation  
• Canva for report design  

Reference resources include guidelines from organizations like OWASP.

---

## Email Analysis Process

1. Collect email samples from various sources
2. Examine sender address and domain
3. Inspect links and attachments
4. Check for urgency or threatening language
5. Identify spoofed or suspicious domains
6. Classify the email risk level

---

## Email Classification

Emails were categorized into three types:

Safe Email  
A legitimate email from a trusted sender with verified domain and no suspicious links.

Suspicious Email  
An email that contains unusual elements but does not clearly confirm phishing.

Phishing Email  
A malicious email designed to steal user information through deceptive links or fake websites.

---

## Phishing Indicators

Common phishing indicators include:

• Spoofed email addresses  
• Fake domains  
• Suspicious or shortened links  
• Urgent or threatening language  
• Unexpected attachments  
• Requests for sensitive information  

---

## Prevention Guidelines

To avoid phishing attacks, users should follow these practices:

• Always verify the sender's email address  
• Do not click unknown or suspicious links  
• Avoid downloading unexpected attachments  
• Enable two-factor authentication  
• Report suspicious emails to the security team
